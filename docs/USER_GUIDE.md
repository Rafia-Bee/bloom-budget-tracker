## Getting Started

### Creating Your Account

1. Visit [bloom-tracker.app](https://bloom-tracker.app)
2. Click **Register** on the login page
3. Enter your email and create a password (minimum 8 characters)
4. Click **Register** to create your account

### First-Time Setup

After registering and logging in, you have two options:

#### Option 1: Set Up Recurring Expenses & Debts First (Recommended)

Before creating your budget, you can:
- Add recurring fixed bills (rent, subscriptions, utilities)
- Add debts you're paying off
- This ensures your budget accounts for these expenses automatically

Then proceed to Option 2.

#### Option 2: Start With Weekly Budget

**Step 1: Get Started**

1. Click **"Get Started"** to open the budget setup wizard
2. Enter your current balances:
   - **Debit Balance**: Your current bank account balance (€)
   - **Credit Card Available**: How much credit you have left to spend (€)
   - **Credit Card Limit**: Your total credit card limit (€)
3. Choose your **Budget Period Start Date**
4. Click **"Next: Review Fixed Bills"**

**Step 2: Review Fixed Bills**

- If you set up recurring fixed bills earlier, they'll appear here
- You can adjust amounts if needed
- If you haven't set up bills yet, this page will be empty
- Click **"Next: Confirm Budget"**

**Step 3: Confirm Budget**

- Preview your 4-week budget breakdown
- See how much you'll have available each week after bills
- Review the weekly allocations
- Click **"Create Budget Plan"** to finalize

The app automatically:

- Divides your available funds across 4 weekly budgets
- Deducts recurring fixed bills from your available balance
- Calculates how much you can spend each week
- Sets up Week 1, 2, 3, and 4 with equal allocations
- Accounts for credit card debt (maxed cards won't be included in budgeting)

## Managing Salary Periods & Weekly Budgets

### Understanding the System

Bloom uses a **4-week salary period** system:

- Each salary period = 4 weeks
- Your money is divided equally across Week 1, 2, 3, and 4
- Each week tracks its own expenses and balance

### Switching Between Weeks

- Click the **week selector** dropdown at the top
- Select Week 1, 2, 3, or 4 to view that week's transactions
- The selector shows: "Week X of 4" with the date range

### Viewing Past Salary Periods

- Use the **Salary Periods selector** to navigate to previous salary periods
- All past weeks and their transactions remain accessible
- Great for tracking spending patterns over time

### Deleting Periods

- You can delete individual weekly budgets or entire salary periods
- **Warning**: This deletes all transactions in that period!
- Access deletion options through the period selector dropdown

## Recurring Expenses

### What Are Recurring Expenses?

Expenses that repeat regularly (rent, subscriptions, gym membership, etc.)

### Creating Recurring Expenses

**Method 1: From Dashboard**
1. Tap **+ button** → **Add Expense**
2. Fill in details
3. Toggle **"Make this recurring"**
4. Set frequency and day
5. Click **Add**
6. **First instance auto-generates immediately!**

**Method 2: From Recurring Expenses Page**
1. Go to **Recurring Expenses** in the navigation
2. Click **"+ Create"** button
3. Fill in details and frequency
4. Click **"Create"**
5. **First instance auto-generates immediately!**

### Managing Recurring Expenses

Each recurring expense has four action buttons (in order):

1. **Fixed Bill**: Toggle whether it's a fixed bill (affects budget calculations)
2. **Edit**: Update name, amount, frequency, category
3. **Pause**: Toggle active/inactive without deleting (paused expenses won't auto-generate)
4. **Delete**: Remove the template (doesn't delete already-generated expenses)

**Additional Features:**

- **⚡ Generate Now**: Manually trigger generation for upcoming instances (if needed)
- First instance auto-generates immediately when creating a new recurring expense

### Frequency Options

- **Weekly**: Choose day of week (e.g., every Monday)
- **Biweekly**: Every 2 weeks on chosen day
- **Monthly**: Choose day of month (1-31)
- **Custom**: Set your own interval (e.g., every 3 days, every 45 days)

## Debt Management

### Adding a Debt

1. Go to **Debts** in the navigation
2. Click **"+ Add Debt"**
3. Fill in the required fields:
   - **Debt Name**: e.g., "Student Loan", "Credit Card"
   - **Current Balance (€)**: How much you currently owe
   - **Original Amount (€)**: The initial debt amount
   - **Monthly Payment (€)**: Your regular payment amount (optional)
4. Click **"Add Debt"**

### Setting Up Recurring Debt Payments

You can automate debt payments in two ways:

**Method 1: From Recurring Expenses Page**

1. Go to **Recurring Expenses** in the navigation
2. Create a recurring expense for your debt payment
3. Set the frequency (usually monthly)
4. This will auto-generate payments each period

**Method 2: From Quick Add**

1. Tap the **+ button** on dashboard
2. Choose **"Add Expense"**
3. Enter your debt payment details
4. Toggle **"Make this recurring"**
5. Set frequency and create

### Making One-Time Debt Payments

**From Dashboard:**

1. Tap **+ button** → **Debt Payment**
2. Select the debt from the list
3. Enter payment amount
4. Choose payment date and method
5. Click **Add**

**From Debts Page:**

1. Find your debt in the list
2. Click **"💰 Pay"** button
3. Enter amount and details
4. Click **Record Payment**

### Tracking Progress

- **Progress bar**: Visual representation of debt payoff
- **Current Balance**: Updates automatically with each payment
- **Original Amount**: Helps you see total progress
- **Payment history**: View all payments in the Debts page

## Import/Export

### Importing Bank Transactions

1. Click on your **username dropdown** (top right corner)
2. Select **Import Bank Transactions**
3. Paste your bank statement (tab or comma-separated)
4. Map columns to match your data:
   - Transaction Date
   - Amount
   - Description
5. Review the preview
6. Click **"Import"**

**Supported format:**

- JSON format with structured data
- Tab-separated (copied from Excel/Google Sheets)
- CSV with proper column mapping

**Note:** Only JSON format is supported for the **Import** menu option. The mapping interface helps you structure your data correctly.

### Exporting Data

1. Click on your **username dropdown** (top right corner)
2. Select **"Import/Export"**
3. Choose what data to export by checking boxes:
   - Budget Periods
   - Transactions
   - Recurring Expenses
   - Debts
   - Settings
4. Select export format:
   - **JSON**: For importing back into Bloom (full data structure)
   - **CSV**: For spreadsheet analysis (flattened data)
5. Click **"Export"**
6. Save the file to your computer

**Export Format Details:**

- **JSON**: Preserves all relationships, can be re-imported
- **CSV**: Easier to analyze in Excel/Google Sheets, cannot be re-imported

## Tips & Best Practices

### 💡 Budget Tips

1. **Start with weekly budgets** if you're new to budgeting - easier to manage
2. **Track every expense** - even small ones add up
3. **Review at the end of each period** - see where your money went
4. **Set realistic goals** - don't make your budget too tight

### 🎯 Recurring Expenses Tips

1. **Add all fixed bills as recurring** - never forget rent or subscriptions
2. **Use the Fixed Bill toggle** - separates non-negotiable expenses
3. **Set end dates** for temporary subscriptions
4. **Review quarterly** - cancel unused subscriptions

### 💳 Credit Card Tips

1. **Set your credit limit** in settings
2. **Track credit card expenses separately** - know your balance
3. **Plan debt payments** - use the debt tracker to pay down balances

### 📊 Category Tips

1. **Be consistent** - use the same categories for similar expenses
2. **Use subcategories** - more detailed insights
3. **Food = Groceries + Eating Out** - separate these for better tracking

### 🔄 Workflow Tips

1. **Add expenses daily** - don't wait until the end of the week
2. **Check dashboard regularly** - stay aware of your spending
3. **Use the draggable + button** - move it out of the way if needed
4. **Create next period in advance** - always have a budget ready

## Troubleshooting

### "No active budget period found"

**Solution**: Create a new budget period that includes today's date.

### Recurring expense didn't generate

**Solution**:
1. Go to Recurring Expenses
2. Click **"⚡ Generate Now"**
3. Check the transaction list

### Forgot password

1. Click **"Forgot Password?"** on login page
2. Enter your email
3. Check your email for reset link
4. Click the link and set a new password

### Balance doesn't match

- Check if all transactions are in the current period
- Verify payment methods (debit vs credit)
- Look for duplicate transactions
